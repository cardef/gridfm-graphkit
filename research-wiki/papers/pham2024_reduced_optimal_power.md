# N-1 Reduced Optimal Power Flow Using Augmented Hierarchical Graph Neural Network

## One-line thesis
AHGNN chains a base-case GNN into a contingency-case GNN, augmented with historical congested-line data, to screen N-1 OPF constraints without rating violations.

## Problem / Gap
N-1 OPF must monitor thermal limits across the base case plus every non-radial single-element contingency, but the great majority of these line-rating constraints are never binding, making the full problem computationally prohibitive for large networks. Prior ML-for-OPF work either ignored topology (DNN/CNN dispatch predictors from load profiles) or, when GNN-based, ignored line-rating limits or left constraint violations in the solution; the authors' own prior GNN-based ROPF work handled only the non-contingency case.

## Method
AHGNN is a nested two-stage GNN pipeline built on the XENet message-passing layer, which updates each node and edge from concatenated incoming/outgoing message stacks (self-attention-weighted over neighbors) through separate MLPs for node and edge updates. Stage one (BC-GNN) classifies congested lines in the pre-contingency base case; its predictions are concatenated as extra input features into stage two (CC-GNN), which classifies congested lines separately for each of the 106 non-radial single-line contingencies of the IEEE 73-bus test system (2 of its 108 branches are radial and excluded, since their removal would island the network). The monitored critical-line set for the N-1 reduced OPF is the union of three sources: BC-GNN predictions, CC-GNN predictions, and notable congested lines (NCL) mined from historical full N-1 OPF solutions. Training uses a custom weighted-factor (WF) cross-entropy loss, Loss = -Σ yᵢ·log(ŷᵢ·WF), that up-weights the class responsible for type-II errors (a truly congested line predicted as uncongested), since only that error type causes constraint violations in the reduced OPF.

## Key Results
- The WF loss cut the type-II (false-negative) error rate from 0.358% to 0.059% (~6x reduction) versus plain cross-entropy, at the cost of type-I error rising from 0.673% to 0.966%; overall error rate stayed roughly flat.
- On 1,000 held-out online samples, AHGNN was the only one of five compared models (NCL-only, GNN, AGNN, HGNN, AHGNN) with zero N-1 line-rating violations across every sample; the other four had per-sample violation counts up to 14 lines, with plain GNN (no BC-GNN augmentation) faring worst.
- AHGNN's N-1 ROPF objective cost averaged 100.061% of the full N-1 OPF cost (median 100.043%, max 100.193%, min 100.002%, std 0.051); ~500/1,000 samples had <0.04% cost increase.
- Solving all 1,000 samples took 7,098.91s with N-1 ROPF vs 8,753.82s with full N-1 OPF (19% faster), even though the constraint count dropped by up to 26% (~84,000 → ~64,000) — a disproportion the authors attribute to the small size of the 73-bus test system.

## Assumptions
- Radial lines (2 of 108 branches in the IEEE 73-bus test system) are excluded from the N-1 contingency set per industry practice, since their outage would island the network — only non-radial contingencies are modeled.
- Training/test data (10,000 + 1,000 samples) are generated by perturbing one base load profile with two random shift parameters (±5% global, ±2% per-bus), not independently sampled operating conditions or topology changes.
- The true critical-line set is assumed recoverable, with high recall, from the union of BC-GNN predictions, CC-GNN predictions, and historically observed notable congested lines (NCL).

## Limitations / Failure Modes
- Evaluated only on the modest IEEE 73-bus system; the authors state the 26% constraint reduction did not translate proportionally into computing-time savings because the system is too small, and explicitly defer the larger-scale (thousands-of-buses) case to future work — no such evaluation exists in this paper.
- Even AHGNN's screened ROPF trades a small solution-quality gap for speed: mean objective cost is 0.061% higher than full N-1 OPF (max 0.193%), attributed to relaxed generation-ramping-rate constraints under the reduced constraint set.
- The four ablation baselines (NCL-only, GNN, AGNN, HGNN) that omit the full three-source integration all produced N-1 ROPF solutions with line-rating violations (up to 14 lines/sample); the paper does not test AHGNN's sensitivity to a smaller/imperfect NCL history or to conditions outside the load-perturbation regime used for both training and test.

## Reusable Ingredients
- XENet-style message passing (separate MLPs for node/edge updates over concatenated incoming/outgoing, self-attention-weighted neighbor messages) as an edge-and-node-aware GNN layer for line-flow-sensitive tasks.
- Weighted-factor (WF) cross-entropy loss for asymmetric-cost binary classification — up-weight the class whose false negatives are structurally more expensive (here, missing a congested line breaks feasibility) — a general recipe for constraint-screening classifiers.
- Nested two-stage prediction (a base-case model's output concatenated as an input feature to a downstream contingency-case model) combined with a historical/statistical prior (NCL) as an ensemble strategy for constraint-set reduction in optimization pipelines.

## Open Questions
- Does the three-source (BC-GNN + CC-GNN + NCL) screening approach scale to systems with thousands of buses, where the authors' own analysis suggests constraint-reduction-to-time-savings gains would be larger — but this is untested here?
- Would AHGNN's zero-violation record hold under topology changes (e.g., the network-reconfiguration OPF named as future work) or genuinely out-of-distribution load conditions, rather than the ±5%/±2% perturbations of a single base profile used for both training and the 1,000-sample online test?

## Abstract (original)

> Optimal power flow (OPF) is used to perform generation redispatch in power system real-time operations. N-1 OPF can ensure safe grid operations under diverse contingency scenarios. For large and intricate power networks with numerous variables and constraints, achieving an optimal solution for real-time N-1 OPF necessitates substantial computational resources. To mitigate this challenge, machine learning (ML) is introduced as an additional tool for predicting congested or heavily loaded lines dynamically. In this paper, an advanced ML model known as the augmented hierarchical graph neural network (AHGNN) was proposed to predict critical congested lines and create N-1 reduced OPF (N-1 ROPF). The proposed AHGNN-enabled N-1 ROPF can result in a remarkable reduction in computing time while retaining the solution quality. Several variations of GNN-based ML models are also implemented as benchmark to demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed AHGNN approach. Case studies prove the proposed AHGNN and the associated N-1 ROPF are highly effective in reducing computation time while preserving solution quality, highlighting the promising potential of ML, particularly GNN in enhancing power system operations.
